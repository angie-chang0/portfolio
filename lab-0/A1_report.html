Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Across 7,478 cases from 2018–2025, fatal overdoses in Allegheny County are concentrated among adults 25–55 with a clear male skew across every race group. Time-series analysis shows a structural regime shift beginning 2014–2016 as fentanyl enters and then dominates the drug supply; counts remain elevated through the pandemic period, even as non-fentanyl deaths decline. Fentanyl is the single largest driver, but the polysubstance picture is crucial: frequent co-occurrences include fentanyl + heroin, fentanyl + cocaine, and pairings with benzodiazepines and alcohol. Demographically, White and Black/African American residents bear most of the burden in absolute numbers, and the fentanyl share is high across most age bands from 20–59 for multiple groups, indicating a widespread synthetic-opioid era rather than a problem confined to a single community. Three lessons stand out: (1) fentanyl is the dominant risk factor, but co-involved drugs amplify lethality; (2) gender and race disparities persist, with nuanced age patterns that matter for targeting outreach (3) trends are temporal and contextual, with spikes aligned to supply shifts and pandemic disruption.
